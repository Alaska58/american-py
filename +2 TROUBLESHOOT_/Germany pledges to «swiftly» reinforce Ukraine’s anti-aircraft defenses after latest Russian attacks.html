The German government has promised Monday to "quickly" reinforce Ukraine's anti-aircraft defenses following the day's attacks by the Russian army on the capital, Kiev, and other cities in the country, which have left around ten dead and dozens wounded.

"We are doing everything possible to quickly strengthen Ukraine's anti-aircraft defense," German Foreign Minister Annalena Baerbock said via her account on the social network Twitter. "Kiev residents fear death as they are caught in morning traffic," she has denounced.

Thus, she has stated that "it is vile and unjustifiable that (Russian President Vladimir) Putin is firing rockets at cities and civilians," after German Defense Minister Christine Lambrecht announced that in the coming days, the first of four state-of-the-art Iris-T SLM air defense systems will be ready for delivery to Ukraine.

"Russia's missile and drone attacks terrorize above all the civilian population," Lambrecht said, as reported by the German news agency DPA.

The attacks have taken place hours after the Russian president accused the Ukrainian secret services of the "terrorist attack" perpetrated on Saturday against the Kerch bridge, which connects Russia with the Crimean peninsula, which left it partially disabled and which resulted in at least three dead.

Putin himself has confirmed during the day a "massive attack" against Ukrainian territory and threatened a "harsh response" against Ukraine if it "continues its acts of terrorism". The Russian Defense Ministry has stressed that the attacks "have achieved their objectives" and emphasized that it attacked energy facilities, command posts and communications centers.
